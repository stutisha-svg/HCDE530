# Design Buddy: MP2 Reflection

## What did you build?

I built Design Buddy as a small Figma plugin that tries to make end-of-day reflection feel less like homework and more like a conversation with your own work. The flow moves through View 1 Anchor [grounding each entry in something real in the file], View 2 Draw [choosing a theoretical lens], View 2b Drawn Prompt [letting the question land before you write], View 3 Reflect [where critique becomes words], View 4 Saved [a pause to continue or open the log], and View 5 Log Hub [where history, compilation, and export live together]. You pick a frame or token, draw from Inspiration, Connections, or Interfacing (Thread opens after three entries), and write in a fixed 360×480 panel. Reflections compile into documents and export to your Notion workspace. Prompts draw on our readings (Calvino, Norman, Berger), pushing toward design theory rather than "how was your day?" Thread entries use `{past_excerpt}` from earlier reflections, which helped me think about continuity across a project. I kept the UI card-based and typographic because the panel is tight and I wanted the experience to feel intentional.

## What decisions did you make?

The biggest choice was building inside Figma at all. Reflection belongs where the work already is; storing a frame preview as `anchorImage` in `clientStorage` keeps each entry tied to something tangible. I folded View 6 [document generation in the brief] into View 5's Documents tab so the log hub stays the one place you return to. Notion export replaced `.md` download after feedback, with a Vercel proxy handling user OAuth so exports land in each person's workspace. I also renamed the View 5 footer to "Export all to Notion," added incremental sync via `notionExportedReflectionIds`, built a quick-log FAB, and replaced a cluttered "Open in Notion" button with sync status on each card. I deferred the AI follow-up (Step 13) because the core loop needed to feel solid first. Privacy copy on Views 1, 3, and 5 [anchor, reflect, log/export] makes the local-first choice visible. Notion auth pivoted more than once, from shared token to user OAuth, then to polling and paste-as-backup once Figma's desktop sandbox blocked browser handoff.

## What would you do differently?

I would design Notion OAuth for Figma desktop on day one instead of retrofitting it. I would also want a two-way Notion connection: export only goes out today, so if you edit a reflection in Notion, View 5 [the log hub] does not know and the two copies drift. Webhooks or a periodic pull would make the log feel alive rather than frozen at export time. On View 3 [reflect], I would tuck long `helper_text` behind a "show context" toggle like View 2b, because rich reading context fights the textarea in a panel that cannot scroll. I wish I had stress-tested storage with real frame PNGs earlier; Break apart restore only broke once documents carried actual image weight.

## What does this work demonstrate?

Looking back, four competencies stand out. **C8 (Building and deploying a complete tool)** is the through-line: I scoped, built, and shipped something for a real HCD use case, and writing this reflection is part of being honest about what held up and what did not. **C1 (Vibecoding and rapid prototyping)** shaped how most of the work got done; I used Cursor to draft and iterate on `ui.html`, `code.js`, and the Vercel proxy, then decided what to keep, rewrite, or cut when the first version failed in Figma desktop. **C4 (APIs and data acquisition)** shows up in Notion export: reading API docs, routing OAuth through a proxy, polling file uploads, and chunking blocks so exports actually reach a user's workspace. **C7 (Critical evaluation and professional judgment)** is what kept the project shippable; I treated AI output as a starting point, verified exports and storage with real data, and pivoted auth UX when I could not trust the original handoff. Design Buddy is not a notes app for designers; it is an attempt to build reflective practice that only makes sense inside Figma.
