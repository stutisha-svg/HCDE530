The document generation logic and card system also involve real computation that benefits from Cursor's code-first environment over a drag-and-drop builder.

The problem

Designers want to reflect on their work but rarely do it well. Documentation feels like a chore, and the moments of real insight — mid-process, between meetings, at the end of a long day — pass without being captured. By the time a post-mortem or retrospective rolls around, the reasoning behind decisions has evaporated.

The result: Figma files full of decisions with no trail of thought. Knowledge that lives only in one person's head. Reflection that never happened, or happened too late.

Who it's for

Mid-level HCD and UX designers working on active projects — self-motivated but time-pressured. They want to reflect but the habit breaks under workload. Design Buddy meets them at the moment they're already at: logging off for the day.

Secondary: design leads who need to hand off project rationale, or designers building a portfolio of thinking over time.

The three reflection decks

Inspiration

Books, films, references, or ideas that informed today's thinking

Connections

Cross-functional decisions, constraints, or relationships considered

Interfacing

What needs to be shared with stakeholders or collaborators

How it works

The buddy lives as a persistent desktop icon — always available, never intrusive. At log-off, it opens a panel and asks the designer to anchor their reflection to a piece of actual work: a selected Figma frame, or pasted text. Then it draws a card from one of the three decks (chosen or random) and poses a focused prompt. The designer responds in their own words. That's it.

Reflections accumulate locally. When ready, the tool generates a well-formatted design document — structured by the three decks, written in the designer's own voice — exported to a Figma page or as Markdown.
